I'm Cliff Harvey co-founder of Functor AB, a Swedish research spinoff leveraging modern advances in programming languages and type systems to disrupt software development.

I have an academic background in physics, with a degree from WPI where I did some quantum information theory research. I continued to closely follow developments in physics, particularly fundamental physics, high-energy particle physics. I'll occasionally post about these and other topics on my Google Plus page.

For several years I have loved to program in strongly-typed pure functional languages like Haskell and especially in dependently-typed languages like our own Functor Scalor.

You can find my code on GitHub.

My psuedonym refers to extended analogues of black holes occurring in higher dimensional gravity theories and string theory.

Open-Source Software Projects

  • Destroid – A space shooter based on the classic Asteroids, embracing a toroidal spacetime topology. Written in the functional reactive programming language Elm. Playable here.
  • 5 Qubits project – An interactive exploration of a 5-qubit proof of the Bell-Kochen-Specker theorem in quantum mechanics, exhibiting the results of research carried out at WPI in which the "observables proof" of Divencenzo & Peres is elaborated to a "states proof".
  • svocab – Swedish vocab – A language quizzing and lookup command line utility. Motivated by the need to practice general Haskell programming. In particular, using monad transformer stacks to handle effects, as well as general monadic idioms. It also shows how to locally manage data files through cabal.

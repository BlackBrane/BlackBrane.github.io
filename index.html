I'm Clifford Scott Harvey. Welcome to my blog!

I have a physics degree from Worcester Polytech, where I did some research in quantum information theory. I'm closely following developments in high energy physics, which I occasionally post about on Google Plus.

I'm now focused on functional programming in strongly typed languages like Haskell, and especially in dependently typed languages, namely Idris.

You can find my code on GitHub.

My chosen psuedonym refers to extended analogues of black holes occurring in higher dimensional gravity theories and string theory.

Computing Projects

  • Probability – Idris library for manipulation and visualization of probability distributions.
  • Matrix, Matrix.Algebraic, Matrix.Numeric – Idris libraries for matrix and vector operations with statically verified vector space dimensionalities.
  • Destroid – A space shooter based on the classic Asteroids, embracing a toroidal spacetime topology. Written in the functional reactive programming language Elm.
  • 5 Qubits project – An interactive exploration of a 5-qubit proof of the Bell-Kochen-Specker theorem in quantum mechanics, exhibiting the results of research carried out at WPI in which the "observables proof" of Divencenzo & Peres is elaborated to a "states proof".
